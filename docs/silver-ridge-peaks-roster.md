## Great Ones

**Mule Deer** — Class 5 · Level 10 Fabled
- Herd management: max-level-5 species — shoot level 3–4 bucks, leave 1–2, stack (leave alive) the level 5s; rest in a tent to cycle need zones. They drink at the lakes, so tent between two close lakes with a lookout on each.
- Fabled coats (Great One only): Cinnamon Stripes, Cobweb Enigma, Dripple Drizzle, Dusky Drift, Milky Way, Petal Puff
- *Honest note:* Silver Ridge works for this grind, but it's **not** the fastest Mule Deer GO map — Askiy Ridge is widely considered easier (smaller spawn area, fewer drink zones). Worth knowing before committing hundreds of kills here.

**Black Bear** — Class 7 · Level 10 Fabled
- Herd management: max-level-9 species — never shoot levels 1–4, stack the level 9s. Predator caller pulls them in; they feed early morning and late night.
- Fabled coats (Great One only): Chestnut, Cream, Glacier (×2), Spirit, Spotted
- Great One reaches 316 kg / 697 lbs (vs. 290 kg regular max).

*Great One spawn odds are unofficial — community estimates run ~0.05–0.6% per eligible animal; treat it as a numbers game, not a guaranteed rate.*

## Accuracy notes
- **Classes, max levels, weights, medal scores, zone times, callers, and coats** come straight from the thehuntercotw structured stat sheet, cross-checked against the COTW Wiki.
- **Weights are max only** — that's the value the game shows when you spot an animal; a verified true minimum isn't published, so I've written them as "up to X" rather than invent a range.
- **Hot spots** are biome-level distributions from the official reserve description plus player-reported spots (Elk "bending forest," Mule Deer lakes by the Wild Turkey Outpost) — reliable as *areas*, not pixel-exact spawn points.
- **Mountain Lion** hits Level 9 but has **no** Great One (same pattern as Yukon's Grizzly).
- **Great One spawn odds** are unofficial community estimates.
- If any zone window differs from what you observe on your own map, flag the species and I'll adjust — individual need zones can vary slightly from the standard pattern.
